"# Chapter 11: Bemi v4.0: Ultra-Bandwidth & Execution Zenith Architecture\n\n## 11.4 Dynamic Core/Thread Fusion (DCF)\n\n### 11.4.1 Resolving the Amdahl's Law serial phase bottleneck\nWhile Bemi's 72-thread SMT architecture delivers massive throughput during highly parallel phases of computation, it faces a physical limitation during serial phases. According to **Amdahl's Law**, the execution speedup of any workload is strictly limited by its sequential fraction:\n$$ \\text{Speedup} = \\frac{1}{(1 - P) + \\frac{P}{S}} $$\nWhere $P$ is the parallel fraction and $S$ is the parallel speedup. During serial phases ($P \\to 0$), the execution speedup is bounded by the performance of a single thread.
